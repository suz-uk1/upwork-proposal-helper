Proposal method

UPH is designed to produce proposals that feel human, specific, and easy to scan. It’s not trying to “sound like AI.”

Core structure

The generator follows the same proven structure every time:

  • Hook (2 lines): restate the client’s problem → state the outcome + approach
  • What I understood: short bullets from the job post (requirements/constraints)
  • Plan (2 phases): Phase 1 reduces risk → Phase 2 delivers
  • Proof: 2 concrete proof bullets (you control these in Settings)
  • Questions: 2–3 questions to start the right conversation
  • Close: one clear next step (what you need from the client)

Why this works

  • Skimmable: short sections + bullets
  • Specific: pulls requirements from the job post
  • Human: you add your own proof + style, instead of generic filler

Styles (Voice Packs)

UPH includes multiple Style presets. Styles change the hook, questions, and close line— so your proposals don’t become one identical “template voice.”

  • Examples: Calm Operator / No‑BS Operator / Craftsperson / Storyteller …
  • Pick one that matches how you actually work

Important note (Upwork rules)

Don’t share personal contact info (email/phone/LinkedIn) in proposals before a contract starts. Keep communication inside Upwork.
